Sam Quill holds a First Class degree in English Language and Literature from the University of Oxford, an M.Phil. from the University of Cambridge, and a Ph.D. from Queen Mary, University of London, where he also taught undergraduate courses and earned a Certificate in Learning and Teaching with Distinction. He is an Associate Fellow of the Higher Education Academy. Since 2019, private tutoring has been his full-time occupation, and he specialises in preparing students for scholarship examinations at major British public schools, as well as Oxbridge entrance, A-level and GCSE tuition, and personal statement support. He also offers the Salon de Conversation, a programme of one-to-one tutorials for adults in literature, philosophy, theology, and history. Beyond teaching, Sam is a published poet, with work in PN Review and the London Review of Books Bookshop Blog among other places; a musician, with recordings on all major streaming services; and a reviewer for the Times Literary Supplement.
